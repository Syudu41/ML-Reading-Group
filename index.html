About MLRG

What We Read

MLRG covers the full breadth of modern machine learning and AI research. Our sessions explore foundational topics including deep learning architectures, natural language processing, computer vision, reinforcement learning, and probabilistic modeling — from seminal works that shaped the field to the latest preprints pushing its frontiers.

We place special emphasis on understanding why methods work, not just how to use them. Papers are selected for their scientific rigor, novelty, and relevance to the group's evolving research interests. Theoretical insights, empirical contributions, and application papers are all in scope.

Our Principles

Rigor Over Hype
We prioritize papers with strong empirical or theoretical foundations, not just trending topics.
Open Discussion
Every voice matters; questions and critiques are encouraged at all levels of expertise.
Breadth & Depth
We span sub-fields to maintain a broad ML horizon while diving deep into each paper's core ideas.
Community First
We build a supportive research community across labs and departments at the University of Memphis.

Who Should Attend

MLRG is open to all University of Memphis graduate students, postdocs, and faculty with an interest in machine learning research. No prior expertise is required — curiosity is the only prerequisite. Sessions are held weekly and typically include a 30-minute presentation followed by an open discussion. Whether you are new to the field or an active researcher, you are welcome here.
