About MLRG

What We Read

The pace of ML research is rapid and unrelenting — papers are the primary medium through which new knowledge is created and shared. MLRG exists to provide the structure, accountability, and collaborative energy needed to keep up. Each week we go beyond skimming: we read deeply, present clearly, and question rigorously.

ML papers are often dense and mathematical. Skimming gives you breadth but risks misunderstanding; deep reading gives you genuine understanding but demands time. Our group encourages both: one paper receives a full presentation and critique each session, while participants are expected to survey a small set of additional papers for breadth. Every session follows a consistent format — 30–40 minutes of presentation, 15–20 minutes of open discussion.

Our Principles

Depth & Breadth
One paper per week receives a full deep-read and presentation. Participants also survey additional papers to maintain a broad horizon across ML sub-fields.
Critical Thinking
We examine the intellectual merits and broader impacts of each paper — evaluating strengths, weaknesses, rigor, reproducibility, and relevance through active discussion and structured critique.
Communication & Citizenship
Constructive participation from all members is expected and valued. We embrace diverse perspectives and the many ways ML research touches different fields and communities.
Consistency & Co-Learning
Building deep knowledge of ML takes consistent and concerted effort. We hold each other accountable and grow together — because we move faster and better as a group.

Who Should Attend

MLRG is open to all University of Memphis graduate students and researchers interested in machine learning. Presentations rotate among group members — every participant is encouraged to lead a session. A designated discussant prepares critiques for each paper. Participants are also expected to survey a small set of additional papers each week (typically 5, or 3 if managing heavy coursework or TA duties), logging a brief summary with strengths and weaknesses for each. All communication and materials are shared through Microsoft Teams.
